Below are the legal rights that you have under the law, and what Pulse does to protect those rights:

The right to be informed

Pulse is publishing this Privacy Policy to keep you informed about what we do with your Personal Data. We strive to be transparent about how we use your data.

The right to access

You have the right to access your information. Our Services give you the ability to access and update or delete certain information about you from within the Service. In certain cases, you may need to contact the administrator of your account or your company’s account. Please contact Pulse at contact@pulsegen.io if you have any questions or wish to access the Personal Data Pulse holds about you.

The right to rectification

If the information Pulse holds about you is inaccurate or not complete, you have the right to ask us to rectify it. If that data has been passed to a third party with your consent or for legal reasons, then we must also ask them to rectify the data. Please contact us at contact@pulsegen.io if you need us to rectify your information.

The right to erasure

This is sometimes called ‘the right to be forgotten’. If you want Pulse to erase all your Personal Data and we do not have a legal reason to continue to process and hold it, please contact us at contact@pulsegen.io.

The right to restrict processing

You have the right to ask Pulse to restrict how we process your data. This means we are permitted to store the data but not further process it. We keep just enough data to make sure we respect your request in the future. If you want us to restrict processing of your data, please contact us at contact@pulsegen.io.

The right to object

You have the right to object to Pulse processing your data even if it is based on our legitimate interests, the exercise of official authority, direct marketing (including data aggregation), and processing for purposes of statistics. If you wish to object please contact us at contact@pulsegen.io.

The right to data portability

If you want Pulse to port your data elsewhere, please contact us at contact@pulsegen.io.

The right to complain to a Supervisory Authority

You have the right to complain to the supervisory authority for data protection. In India, this is the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, Government of India. You can also complain to the European Supervisory Authority in the country where you live, work, or where you believe a breach has happened.

Have Questions?

Why Pulse? What can I use it for? What is an insight?

Pulse is ideal for SaaS Product Teams seeking to streamline customer feedback and market analysis, enabling scalable execution of business objectives.

Unlike platforms like Mixpanel, which primarily offer insights from platform analytics (e.g., monthly active users, user journey, etc.), Pulse integrates various data sources, including support tickets and CRM data, providing comprehensive insights for product decision-making.

While Mixpanel excels at post-implementation analysis, Pulse empowers Product Managers (PMs) to make informed decisions before development. It offers data points such as customer demand, cumulative ARR, existing alternative solutions, competitor features, trend analysis, and alignment with strategic goals.

Additionally, Pulse not only assists in backlog prioritization but also fosters innovation by inspiring new feature ideas and keeping PMs informed about market trends and competitor activities.

Which data sources do you support? Do you also provide support for integrations?

We offer seamless integration with popular internal platforms such as Zendesk, Freshdesk, JIRA, Slack, Salesforce, and more. If customers intend to use custom integrations, the platform will be flexible enough to configure them.

Pulse requires access to diverse data sources, including support system data, to correlate bug/feature reports with ROI and CRM data, enabling the identification of customer needs. The more the data, the better the insights.

Additionally, Pulse integrates with external sources to retrieve relevant product trend data from sites like G2, Product Hunt, Tech Crunch, etc. We also collaborate with data aggregation platforms to deliver insights on market trends and competitor strategies. You can customize external source preferences in the platform’s admin panel.

Which external sources does your platform support, and how does your feed differ from my LinkedIn feed, which already provides relevant posts based on my interests?

Pulse scours the web daily to identify your closest competitors, ensuring you're always up-to-date with industry innovations. With Pulse, you receive comprehensive market research, preventing you from discovering competition late in your development process.

We provide these results as you build your product, saving you time on independent research. Pulse not only keeps you informed about new competition but also updates you on existing competitors and new features they release. Our external insights cover dynamic market changes, customer sentiments, and regulatory threats, enabling informed decision-making. By analyzing this data and correlating it with your backlog, Pulse facilitates data-driven prioritization. In summary, while you could spend countless hours Googling this information yourself, Pulse delivers relevant insights in minutes, tailored to your product line.

Who are Pulse's competitors?

While existing tools rely solely on internal data sources like customer feedback, Pulse recognizes that “innovation requires insights from beyond the company's walls”. By gathering information from diverse external sources, Pulse delivers real-time, relevant content to users. Its unique value proposition lies in its ability to blend internal and external insights, providing valuable data points for more informed product decisions.

Which function at an organization gets the most value from Pulse?

While our primary focus is assisting Product Managers in building better products through insights from both external and internal data, Pulse is also valuable for CXOs and GTM teams seeking a deeper understanding of the product landscape. CXOs can utilize Pulse as a quick snapshot to gain insights into product features, customer pain points, competitor analysis, and market outlook.

How will Pulse handle multiple SKUs/Product Lines within our company?

Each product line has its own separate workspace. PMs can access specific workspaces based on their roles and responsibilities, configured using RBAC within the platform. Each workspace contains insights relevant to its respective product line.

Is Hypothix just a co-pilot? Or Is it just a wrapper on top of GPT?

Hypothix serves as a "What-If" playground for Product Managers, allowing them to simulate feature development's potential impact without actual implementation. It's a data-driven simulator that predicts various scenarios based on specified parameters, providing probabilistic models and supporting data. This is particularly valuable for companies managing numerous feature requests with limited engineering resources. Internally, Hypothix employs pre-trained prompts to generate meaningful responses, though ongoing research efforts aim to enhance its capabilities in this area.
